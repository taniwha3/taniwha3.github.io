Progressive Metal Guitar Curriculum

Created on: 2025-02-27 | Last Updated: 2025-02-27

This section contains a comprehensive 10-year curriculum for mastering progressive metal guitar techniques. Each year builds upon the previous skills, gradually advancing from basic techniques to virtuoso-level complexity.

Practice Guidelines

For detailed practice guidelines, assessment points, and progression metrics, please refer to the main index.

Curriculum Files

Year-by-Year Learning Path

  • Year 1: Foundations - Basic techniques including power chords, palm muting, alternate picking, blues and pentatonic scales through classic rock songs
  • Year 2: Bridging to Intermediate - Thrash metal techniques, hybrid picking, odd time signatures, and progressive rock chord voicings
  • Year 3: Intermediate Mastery - Sweep picking, tapping, all seven modes, neoclassical elements, and composition exercises
  • Year 4: Advanced Proficiency - Polyrhythms, extended range techniques, melodic minor theory, and multi-section compositions
  • Year 5: Advanced Mastery I - 8-string techniques, complex polyrhythms, jazz fusion elements, and extended compositions
  • Year 6: Advanced Mastery II - Endurance building, advanced variations on previous techniques, and performance preparation
  • Year 7: Advanced Mastery III - Extreme polyrhythms, metric modulation, microtonality, and extended improvisation
  • Year 8: Advanced Mastery IV - Professional-level performance, contrapuntal tapping, and sophisticated thematic development
  • Year 9: Advanced Mastery V - Near-virtuoso level techniques, magnum opus compositions, and extended performance stamina
  • Year 10: Virtuoso & Mastery - Complete technical integration, definitive performances, and professional-level composition
